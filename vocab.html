Vocabulary

You can't memorize every word the test might use. What you can do is learn how to work out a word from the sentence around it, and learn the word parts that show up again and again. Those two skills work on words you've never seen.

Working out a word from context

  1. Read the whole sentence, and the one before it. What is it about?
  2. Cover the word (or the blank) and say your own plain word that would fit. Make a prediction first.
  3. Match your prediction to the closest choice. Then check the tone — is the word positive, negative, or neutral? Pick the one that fits both the meaning and the tone.
The path was so narrow that the hikers had to walk single file.
Context tells you the word means "thin" — even if you'd never seen it.

Word parts that unlock meaning

Most long words are built from smaller parts. Learn these and you can often guess a new word by breaking it apart. Inconceivable = in- (not) + conceive (imagine) + -able (can be) = "cannot be imagined."
